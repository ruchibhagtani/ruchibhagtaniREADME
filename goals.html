1. Reduce Violence

  • disrupts communities, hinders economic development, and creates long-term social scars. This goal addresses all forms of violence, including armed conflict, domestic violence, and street crime.

  • Challenges: Reducing violence requires addressing root causes like poverty, inequality, lack of education, and weak law enforcement. It also involves dealing with organized crime, conflict zones, and areas where the rule of law is weak.

2. End Abuse and Exploitation

  • Children are among the most vulnerable in society and are often targets of abuse, exploitation, and trafficking. This goal seeks to protect children from these harms and ensure their rights are respected.

  • Challenges: Children can be exploited in many forms, including forced labor, sexual exploitation, child marriage, and recruitment into armed groups. These issues are often hidden and can be difficult to address due to cultural, economic, and legal barriers.

3. Develop Effective, Accountable Institutions

  • Institutions are the backbone of governance, providing services, ensuring the rule of law, and \implementing policies. For institutions to be trusted and effective, they must be accountable to the people and operate transparently.

  • Challenges:In many countries, institutions may be weak, poorly managed, or plagued by corruption. There may also be a lack of public participation in decision -making processes, leading to a disconnect between the government and the people.

4. Promote Rule of Law

  • The rule of law ensures that laws are applied fairly and consistently, protecting individuals' rights and maintaining social order. Equal access to justice means that all individuals, regardless of their background, have the ability to seek and obtain legal remedies.

  • Challenges:In many regions, legal systems are underfunded, corrupt, or biased, leading to unequal treatment and a lack of trust in the system. In some countries, laws may be used to suppress certain groups or political opposition.
